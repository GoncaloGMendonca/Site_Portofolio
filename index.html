Project Details

Hey there! I'm excited to share with you about my latest project, Reset The Sun, which I've been working on for the past 6-7 months with a group of school colleagues. As a Character Animator/Modeler, Environment Artist, and Trailer Producer/Editor, I was in charge of creating the visual elements for the trailer, and I had a blast doing it!


Reset The Sun is a 2.5D side-scroller game that offers an immersive narrative-based experience, where the story is slowly revealed through objects and marks left behind by people. It's a game that challenges the player to solve puzzles by controlling time.


Creating the protagonist
As the Character Animator/Modeler, my journey began with creating the main protagonist of the game. At first, the character was just a blank slate, so I played around with a few different concepts, ranging from a more artistic/mythical version to a more realistic one.

Ultimately, the team decided on a more realistic version, and to bring it to life, I used Stable Diffusion and other AI technologies like Dall-e to experiment and push my abilities in other areas. I'm not a professional illustrator or drawer, but I wanted to try out these emerging tools and see what I could create.


The backstory of the character is simple, yet intriguing. He's the son of a scientist who accidentally sends himself to the future by pressing the wrong button.

With the concept art and backstory completed, I started modeling the character. The style of the game required a low-poly approach, which presented a challenge for me, but I'm proud of the end result. After the modeling, I moved on to animating the character.

This was my second time working on human character animation, and it was tough, especially the infinite looping animations like walking and running. However, the idle animations were much easier, and I found myself enjoying them more.


Designing environment materials
Aside from the character, I also helped create materials for the game's environment. I was tasked with designing a poster and screen saver for assets inside the game Using Stable Diffusion, I was able to create a poster in just a couple of hours, from an idea in my head to a finished material in the game. The screen saver was a little easier, as the team wanted a "Matrix-like" effect, which I was able to achieve through materials.


Trailer Production Challenge
Finally, the trailer. This was a challenging yet exciting part of the project, as I had to use radio/TV audio and game clips and gameplay to create a mysterious atmosphere and showcase the game. I reused a few clips from a different school assignment, but the main concept was to show the status of the world in the game.

One of the most challenging sequences in the trailer was showcasing a game mechanic through a one-shot sequence where parts of the scenario break and reconstruct in different times. It required a lot of work to set up in Unreal, but the end result was worth it.

Social Media
I had the fantastic opportunity to be part of the marketing team for 'Reset the Sun' as part of a university class project. Our mission? Well, it was pretty straightforward: create a buzz around this game , connect with fellow gamers, and ignite the excitement.


Let me break it down for you: at the heart of our social media campaign was short videos that offered a sneak peek into the game's mesmerizing lore. We knew that to truly do justice to 'Reset the Sun,' our content had to be as mysterious and intriguing as the game itself. We kicked things off with teasers that dropped hints about the mind-bending puzzles and the gripping storyline. Now, speaking of platforms, we dove into Instagram, Twitter, TikTok, and YouTube, Facebook. Want to explore 'Reset the Sun' further? Head over to our social media pages and join the adventure!


Creating Puzzles
I also had the privilege of contributing to the game's puzzles. Designing these puzzles was an absolute delight, and they turned out to be a lot of fun. However, it wasn't without its fair share of challenges. 'Reset the Sun' prides itself on its deliberately simple mechanics, which added an intriguing layer of complexity to the puzzle creation process. This meant that we had to think outside the box to ensure that these puzzles not only entertained players but also offered a satisfying level of difficulty. Additionally, we had to ensure they integrated seamlessly with the game's scenes and the time reversal mechanics.

Smaller Tasks
As the project progressed, I undertook the task of restructuring all the materials, optimizing them to create more efficient master materials. This optimization significantly lighten the load on the game engine, ensuring smoother performance. Moreover, I lent a hand to the team by creating a set of small 3D models that proved invaluable in various aspects of our project. Towards the project's completion, I dedicated my efforts to finalize the game's environments by incorporating additional 3D models. This not only enhanced the overall aesthetic but also ensured that every corner of our virtual world was meticulously crafted and immersive.


Final Chapter
Wrapping up my journey with 'Reset the Sun,' I'm incredibly proud of what our team has achieved over these past months. From shaping the main character and designing environments to crafting puzzles and managing the marketing, it's been an adventure filled with creativity and challenges. I invite you to play 'Reset the Sun.' Check our social media channels to see the teasers and trailers that we've created. Thank you for joining us on this adventure, and I look forward to sharing more exciting projects in the future.

Project Description

For a school project, I was tasked with creating a video typography based on animated text. The focus was on creating a visual composition, including colors, textures, backgrounds, and rhythm, that would accompany the audio and convey the expressiveness of the chosen text. The technical and visual approach was left up to me to decide.


Series Synopsis
Better Call Saul is a popular American TV series created by Vince Gilligan and Peter Gould. It's a prequel to Breaking Bad, and follows the story of Jimmy McGill, a lawyer who eventually transforms into a notorious criminal lawyer named Saul Goodman. The show stars Bob Odenkirk as Saul Goodman, and features a talented cast including Jonathan Banks and Rhea Seehorn.


Video Breakdown
To create a video I knew I had to break down a 3-minute scene into smaller clips so I could work on each clip individually. This made it easier to focus on the details and create the final product.

After downloading the episode from the internet, I used HandBrake to convert the file from .mkv to .mp4. This allowed me to easily import the video into Premiere, where I used the Scene Edit Detection tool to automatically cut the scene into separate clips.

From there, I exported the clips to After Effects and created separate compositions for each one. I used the Rotobrush 2.0 tool to create "plates" of the characters and other elements in the scene, which involved separating the characters from the background. This was the most time-consuming part of the process, as I had to do this frame by frame. However, using the Rotobrush 2.0 tool made the process more efficient and allowed me to have more flexibility in my ideas.

Once the plates were created, I began working on the text animations. I chose fonts and colors that matched those used in the series to ensure consistency. I placed the text in different planes and created the illusion that the words were part of the scene.

Finally, I put everything together in a final composition and checked for consistency and errors before rendering the video and delivering it. This process was time-consuming, but I'm happy with the final product.

Project Description

For our school project, we've been tasked with recreating and animating a work of abstract geometric art using After Effects. Each of us will choose a piece of art (either from the suggested options or one of our own choosing) and study its visual characteristics in order to bring it to life through animation in After Effects.


I chose to recreate Sol LeWitt's series of five paintings featuring distorted, elongated, and compressed cubes in a mannerist study of perspective and form. Born in 1928 in the United States, Sol LeWitt played a crucial role in the conceptual art movement and the transition from modern to postmodern era. He introduced a new dimension to the role of an artist through his intellectual and pragmatic approach to conceptual art.


Breakdown of the video
To recreate the series of five paintings, I divided the project into three parts: the cubes, the border frame, and the final composition. First, I studied how to create the cube itself, as the original artwork featured multiple cubes in different sizes and scales. After watching some tutorials, I learned how to make a 3D cube using square shape layers.


To replicate the cubes, I simply created six faces of the same size, transformed the 2D shapes into 3D shapes, and adjusted them to form a cube. I could manipulate the cube's size and shape by moving the pivot point and rotating the faces already adjacent to each other. Then, I created a null object and positioned it at the center of the cube, giving it a 3D pivot point. Finally, I parented the selected faces to the null object, and I had a 3D cube. I could rotate, stretch, and modify it however I wanted. I also added a camera to achieve consistency between the four cubes.


The second part of the project was to create the moving lines within the frame. After some research and testing, I found it easiest to use paths with the pen tool. I animated the lines, replicating the various shapes the cubes are displayed in the original artwork. I created a shape key for the border frame, which I adjusted to fit the composition. Although there was a slight error where the shape key did not align with the edges of the composition, I decided to keep it in the final product as it added a unique touch.


Overall, recreating Sol LeWitt's artwork was a fascinating experience, and I am proud of the final result. It was challenging to recreate his artistic vision, but it helped me improve my skills and gain a deeper appreciation for his work.

Project Description

OMG I am Late is a 3D endless runner where you control Late Guy, a young man who is late for work. Your objective is to arrive on time by choosing the best route through the city and solving puzzles to overcome obstacles on the way.


The idea for the game came to me while I was on my way to school, and I immediately came up with the name "OMG I am Late." As I walked, I thought about all the times I was running late for something and had to navigate a messy city full of obstacles like parked cars and busy pedestrians. I decided this scenario would make for a great game, where the player could experience the frustration and excitement of trying to make it to their destination on time.


I started drafting ideas and came up with a gameplay concept where the obstacles would be quick-time events that the player must complete in a few seconds. I chose to make the game for phones because the touch screen allows for more creative puzzles. I then created some mock-ups of the UI to get a better sense of how the game would look and feel.


To give myself a better representation of the ideas, I used Blender to render the game's environment and characters. I drew inspiration from Jet Set Radio's graphic style, classic comic books' illustrations and colors, and Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse's animation, directing, and visual style.


As the game's designer and leader, I had a lot of pressure, and it was my first time modeling a human character, which was challenging. However, I was proud of the end result as it had a unique personality that matched the game's style. Modeling other objects like roads, signs, and buildings was also fun, and I enjoyed creating them with the low-poly aesthetic.


I textured the models, drawing inspiration from Dreamcast games to make the textures pop and feel unique. Then, I animated the character, which was also my first time doing so. I learned a lot and gained experience in a new field of 3D art.


Lastly, I created the trailer, animating a shot of a comic book flipping through its pages. Although the end result of the game was lacking, I enjoyed the learning experience, from the technical aspects of modeling and texturing to working as a team and being a leader. I still love the idea and may work on something related to it in the future.

Project Overview

As a creative person always looking for new ways to express myself, I recently took up Substance Painter as a new tool to add to my arsenal. After some brainstorming, I decided to create a knife design using stable diffusion to explore different ideas until I settled on a final concept.


I then used Blender to model the knife with my concept as a reference. It was a smooth and fast process that helped me achieve the desired design quickly.


Next, I focused on texturing the knife in Substance Painter. While the software was user-friendly with a lot of features, it still required some tutorials to master. However, I was able to create the base of the texture quite easily.


Once the texturing was complete, I wanted to test how the pipeline would work with Unreal, which I hope to use for future projects. Unfortunately, exporting and importing textures between the two programs proved to be a bit challenging. I encountered a bug where the alpha of the image affected the texture in Unreal, but not in Substance Painter. I had to find a way to make the model look consistent in both programs.


Finally, I did a small render of my finished knife design. It was a fun project that taught me a lot about Substance Painter, but I know I still have more to learn to improve my skills with the software.

Project Overview

I had the pleasure of experimenting with a fascinating program called Ebsynth. With its unique features and capabilities, I was able to achieve some incredible results that I'm excited to share with you.


One of my favorite experiments involved combining stable diffusion with Ebsynth. By providing an image as the first frame of the video, I let Ebsynth do its magic and track the frame to the video. The end result was a stunning combination of the two that left me in awe.


Another interesting render I explored was combining Faceapp with Ebsynth. As you may know, Faceapp is an app that allows you to manipulate pictures of people and alter details such as age and smile. By integrating it with Ebsynth, I was able to create an "old person filter" in no time at all. While there were a few downsides to this app, it proved to be an invaluable tool in my arsenal.


Overall, I highly recommend giving Ebsynth a try if you're looking for a user-friendly program that produces stunning results. It's been an exciting journey experimenting with this software, and I can't wait to see what other projects I can create with it in the future.

Project Details

Embarking on this project was initially a school assignment, challenging us to create an AR experience. My initial choice was Unreal Engine 5, a platform I was comfortable with due to my previous experience. However, it didn't take long for the technical intricacies, including the integration with Android SDK and other roadblocks, to turn it into a bit of a puzzle. Even Epic's templates couldn't fully untangle the web of challenges I faced.


That's when I stumbled upon Meta Spark, a AR tool and face effect creator crafted by Meta. While it has its limitations and may lack some of the advanced features that Unreal Engine 5 offers, it proved to be a valuable asset for simpler tasks and gave me the means to bring my AR project to life.

The AR Experience
The heart of this AR project revolves around a 3D scene featuring two zombies, accessible via a QR code located in the image gallery on the left. What makes it truly interactive is that a simple touch on the screen triggers a punch or kick animation within the scene. It's worth noting that this isn't a full-fledged game, given the limitations of the software, but it adds a playful and engaging element to the experience.

All in all, it turned out to be an enjoyable and enlightening experiment with AR technology. It provided me with valuable insights into the possibilities and challenges of crafting AR content. If you're curious to witness this effect in action, I've shared a video demonstration on my Instagram page.

As I continue to explore the realm of augmented reality, I look forward to taking on more projects that push the boundaries of this exciting technology. Stay tuned for more adventures on the horizon!

Project Overview

Another school project is underway, and this time, our creative mission is to craft a captivating VR experience from scratch. After some brainstorming, I was struck by an idea that resonated with me – recreating iconic moments from the history of the Beatles. With this inspiring concept in mind, I embarked on the meticulous planning and execution process.


For this ambitious endeavor, I teamed up with a fellow student who brought a different set of skills to the table. While I delved deep into the visual aspects and overall design, my partner took the reins on audio and provided invaluable support with coding for our VR environment. Given our limited time and the need to acquire VR development skills from the ground up within Unreal Engine, we wisely decided to keep the project scope manageable.


Our VR journey would unfold across four distinct scenes, each encapsulating a pivotal chapter in the Beatles' illustrious history:

Scenes
1. English Pub – Serving as the nexus
2. Abbey Road
3. 1969 Rooftop Concert
4. Apple Studio


The core mechanic of our experience was elegantly simple – a "walking simulator." This choice allowed us to channel our energies into recreating the environments authentically, immersing users in the captivating world of the Beatles.

With a well-thought-out plan in hand, I embarked on the rewarding journey of learning the intricacies of VR development. To my pleasant surprise, Unreal Engine proved to be an excellent companion on this quest. Its comprehensive templates and seamless compatibility with various VR headsets made the learning curve smoother than expected.

Fortuitously, our university provided us with a VR headset, an invaluable asset that enabled us to thoroughly test and fine-tune our creation.


As I ventured into the process of creating the scenes, I turned to valuable asset resources such as BlenderKit and Quixel Megascans to acquire the necessary 3D models and textures. While my initial vision aimed for a one-to-one representation, the constraints of time and the availability of pre-made assets led us to craft a faithful "replica" that captured the essence and spirit of each iconic location.

In the realm of VR, audio is paramount to achieving an immersive experience. To this end, we integrated binaural 3D audio, a feat masterfully executed by my talented colleague. His work transformed stereo sound into an immersive audio format, ensuring that users felt genuinely present in each scene.


The logic of navigation within our VR masterpiece was meticulously considered. Our journey commenced at the legendary Abbey Road, and as we strolled along the street, we encountered a charming Beatles Pub. Stepping inside, users encountered a jukebox that allowed them to seamlessly transition between the different exterior scenes mentioned earlier. This flawless switch was achieved through Unreal Engine level streaming, a feature you can witness in action via a video demo placed in the image gallery on the left.

With the audio and scenes now seamlessly intertwined, only the gameplay remained to be fine-tuned. Fortunately, this phase proved to be swift and efficient.


Overall, this project was a delightful and intellectually stimulating endeavor. It offered a unique opportunity to blend creativity and technology, and it was a joy to bring the Beatles' history to life in a virtual world.
